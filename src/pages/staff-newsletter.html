We are a Scent free Workplace!

Let’s join the movement and create a healthier workspace! The new Scent Free Workplace Policy can be found in Dropbox!

Removing unnecessary scented products from where you work (and live) can be beneficial for your health, the health of those around you, and the environment. Chemicals used in fragrances can cause health problems such as shortness of breath, headaches and migraines, nausea, muscle pain, and cold-like symptoms. Asthma, emphysema, bronchitis, and allergies can all be adversely affected by the chemicals found in scented products. According to the Lung Association, one study found that 72 per cent of people with asthma had adverse reactions to perfumes.

Upcoming workshops: Mental Health in the Workplace

Mark your calendars: Dec 12, 2016/ 12:00-3:30pm/ CP1 & 2 Boardrooms.


We have invited Victoria Maxwell to join us and lead a discussion around Mental Illness in the Workplace. At the end of this workshop, you will be able to understand more about the most common mental health issues such as depression, anxiety, and etc, while recognizing typical associated symptoms.

Moreover, you’ll get a chance to recognize the behaviours which are often exhibited at work and are therefore expected. You will also be able to understand the importance of taking a proactive approach to mental health and how the Foundation will support you!

Mental health issues are often misunderstood and stigmatized, and are growing at a faster rate than any other kind of disability in today’s workplace. According to the Canadian Mental Health Association in any given year 1 in 5 Canadians will experience a mental health problem or illness.

Each week approximately 500,000 Canadians don’t feel well enough to go to work due to a mental illness. The Canadian economy spends more than $51 Billion a year in lost productivity due to a workplace mental illness with 70% of overall disability costs attributed to mental health related issues.
