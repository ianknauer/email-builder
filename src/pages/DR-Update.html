I am delighted to share an update on the progress of the Joseph & Rosalie Segal & Family Health Centre. Final completion of the building continues to develop at a rapid pace and we are happy to report it is on track for the official opening and occupancy mid-August 2017. Thanks to your tremendous philanthropic support, this new building is coming to fruition.

The Joseph & Rosalie Segal & Family Health Centre will offer much needed mental health and addictions support to patients and families in our communities. With 100 private rooms, each with their own washroom, this modern space will be the largest purpose-built facility of its kind in the province for people with mental health and substance use issues.


The eight-storey building will consolidate inpatient and outpatient services under one roof to provide clients with the treatment, programs and skills they need to successfully resume life in the community. The facility will be a nurturing, welcoming and healing environment with outdoor spaces, lots of natural light throughout the building, a gym facility, and multiple spaces to visit with family. The environment will have a positive impact on patients and their families.



Left to right: MLA Sam Sullivan; Kip Woodward, VCH Chair; Mr Joseph Segal; Minister Terry Lake; Dr Thea Gilks; Barbara Grantham, VGH & UBC Hospital Foundation

On March 28, 2017 the Joseph & Rosalie Segal & Family Health Centre opened its doors to media for a preview of the facility. Members of government were present to announce the substantial completion of the building. Speakers at this event included Minister Terry Lake, MLA Sam Sullivan and also Joseph Segal. Mr Segal attended with his wife, Rosalie, and their family members.

The Foundation will be hosting a donor celebration event for the building opening and we hope you can join us. Please save the date for August 16, 2017. An invitation with details will be sent to you in the near future. The Joseph & Rosalie Segal & Family Health Centre is vitally important to mental health patients and their families and we thank you for your passion, support and unwavering commitment to this cause. You are truly making a difference to so many lives in British Columbia.

Sincerely.

VGH & UBC Hospital Foundation
