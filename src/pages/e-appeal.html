Dear [[S1:first_name]],

I have no memory of the accident and I’m told that’s probably a good thing. I was riding my bike to work on a Thursday morning last January when a bus collided with me—and then I was hit by a dump truck.

My first memory of that day is waking up in the Intensive Care Unit (ICU) at Vancouver General Hospital (VGH) with a team of doctors and nurses around me. I was suffering from severe lower limb trauma and they had a very short amount of time to save my leg…and I’m so happy to report that they did!

I never thought I would need emergency help from VGH—until that scary day when I wouldn’t have survived without it. Thanks to donors like you, I received incredible care from the moment I was admitted to VGH and throughout my recovery at UBC Hospital and GF Strong Rehab Centre.

You and I and all of our fellow British Columbians are lucky to have these health care centres in case the unexpected and life-threatening happens.

Please donate to support the team of people who got me up and walking on my own two legs again.

Sincerely.


Richard
Grateful Patient

PS. If you'd like to learn more about my story, please visit vghfoundation.ca/richard.
